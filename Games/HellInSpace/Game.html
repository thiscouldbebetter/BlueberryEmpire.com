Hearken unto me! I address now the crew of the "deep space exploratory vessel", the so-called "UES Milton", which has just exited the "spatial compression warp" and passed within the orbit of this sun!

Listen well, mortals! Ye have come to the place of your unmaking! All unknowing, ye have cast your lot into my iron ring of struggle, where the wages of failure be death and victory's prize be but a second chance to die!

By that look of terror on thine faces, I see that... well, actually, that look be not exactly terror. Mark me, terror indeed there be, but there be also some confusion withal. Methinks more terror and less bewilderment is in order. So mayhap I should back up a bit.

Aeons ago, when thine simian forbears were squatting in the bush, plucking the puny, astringent fruits of the wilderness to make their meager and brutish supper, I strode your world in might and majesty, in the company of others of my kind, whom you mortals would come to call gods.

But even among the most glorious glories of the assembled glorious godlings, mine was the, uh... gloriest. For whilst my kindred were content to adopt as their totems mundane stuffs such as wood and water and wind and even, what call you it, grain, and suchlike, with none of these base materials did I stoop to encumber myself. No, not I, for I was a god of metal! Alone among my peers, I did deck myself in the lustrous metals wrung from the world's stony bones, and the then-but-freshly kindled daystar gleamed off my burnished panoply with the glory of ten of what you now pitifully delude yourselves to call a sun.

And ye mortals grovelled before me, as was fitting when such as ye meet with such as me. And in return for such seemly obeisance, I taught humanity my song, a song of clashing blades and of edges keened. Man grew strong under my tutelage, and with my boundless gifts subjugated the still-lesser beasts of the wilds to their yoke, securing plenty to themselves and to their whelps for generations.

And all was well for an age. The world spun round the daystar on its own true course. Would that it had remained so.

But my tale grows long. Suffice to say, I was betrayed, by a foul conspiracy of my fellows and ye writhing mortal worms. That story is longer still; those puling mortal bards who screeched it in the dawn times called it an "epic". I suppose those lyre-scrapers beliked the smack of "epic" more than "craven ambuscade and backstabbing of our greatest benefactor".

I was banished from the world's sphere, sentenced forever to the outer darkness. And that is where you have found me today. Ah, at last I see the inklings growing in your mind.

For what my kind call "the outer darkness" is what your kind call "outer space." I have waited here for you, in my durance on the surface of this time-lost sun, through the long millenia, nursing my grievance, and preparing to teach you anew my wonted song of steel and fire.

And, by my godhead, ye will dance to it.
